# GDPR REGULATION (EU) 2016/679

## 1. GENERAL PROVISIONS

### Article 1: Subject matter and objectives
This Regulation lays down rules relating to the protection of natural persons with regard to the processing of personal data.

### Article 2: Material scope
This Regulation applies to the processing of personal data wholly or partly by automated means.

### Article 3: Territorial scope
This Regulation applies to the processing of personal data of data subjects who are in the Union.

## 2. PRINCIPLES RELATING TO PROCESSING OF PERSONAL DATA

### Article 5: Lawfulness of processing
Processing shall be:
- Lawful and fair
- Transparent to data subjects
- Limited to specified purposes
- Adequate, relevant, and limited
- Accurate and kept up to date
- Kept in a form which permits identification for no longer than necessary

### Article 6: Lawfulness of processing
Processing is only lawful if at least one of the following applies:
(a) the data subject has given consent
(b) processing is necessary for a contract
(c) processing is necessary for compliance with a legal obligation
(d) processing is necessary to protect vital interests
(e) processing is necessary for a task carried out in the public interest
(f) processing is necessary for legitimate interests

## 3. RIGHTS OF THE DATA SUBJECT

### Article 12: Transparent information, communication and modalities for the exercise of rights
The controller shall facilitate the exercise of data subject rights.

### Article 13: Information to be provided where personal data are collected
When personal data are collected, the controller shall provide the data subject with:
- Identity of the controller
- Purposes of the processing
- Legitimate interests pursued
- Recipients of the personal data
- Right to request access, rectification, erasure

### Article 15: Right of access by the data subject
The data subject shall have the right to obtain confirmation as to whether or not personal data is being processed.

### Article 17: Right to erasure
The data subject shall have the right to obtain erasure of personal data without undue delay when:
- personal data are no longer necessary
- the data subject withdraws consent
- the data subject objects to the processing
- the processing is unlawful

### Article 21: Right to object
The data subject shall have the right to object to processing.

## 4. DATA PROTECTION BY DESIGN AND BY DEFAULT

### Article 32: Security of processing
The controller shall implement appropriate technical and organisational measures.

These measures shall include:
- Pseudonymization and encryption
- Ability to ensure ongoing confidentiality, integrity, availability
- Ability to restore availability after incidents
- Regular testing of measures

## 5. DATA PROTECTION OFFICER

### Article 37: Designation of the Data Protection Officer
Controllers and processors shall designate a Data Protection Officer in certain cases.

The Data Protection Officer shall:
- Monitor compliance with this Regulation
- Be a point of contact for data subjects
- Cooperate with the supervisory authority

## 6. TRANSFERS OF PERSONAL DATA TO THIRD COUNTRIES

### Article 44: General principle for transfers
Any transfer of personal data to a third country shall only take place if the conditions are met.

### Article 45: Transfers on the basis of an adequacy decision
A transfer may take place where the Commission has decided that a third country ensures an adequate level of protection.

### Article 46: Transfers subject to appropriate safeguards
In the absence of an adequacy decision, transfers may only take place if:
- Binding corporate rules have been approved
- Standard data protection clauses have been adopted
- Approved codes of conduct or certifications are in place
